What a field sales app needs to do

Field reps have simple requirements. They need to know where to go, how to get there, and a fast way to record what happened. Any field sales app worth installing should cover five core functions:

  1. See accounts on a map. Reps need a visual view of their territory. Pins on a map, color-coded by status or priority, with the ability to filter and search. If a meeting cancels, the rep should be able to spot nearby accounts in seconds.
  2. Plan and optimize routes. Compare the time spent sequencing stops manually with the route workflow during the trial.
  3. Log visits and check-ins. Use a GPS-assisted proximity check, check-out, notes, outcomes, and next steps. The result should be quick to record between stops.
  4. Access CRM data in the field. Contact history, deal stage, last interaction, open tasks. Reps should not have to call the office to look up an account. The app either connects to your CRM or acts as one.
  5. Report back to managers. Activity dashboards, visit counts, territory coverage. Managers need visibility without asking reps to fill out spreadsheets at the end of the day.

No product covers these jobs in the same way. Some are separate field-sales platforms with CRM sync. Others use the records and permissions already configured in Salesforce.

6 field sales apps compared

Badger Maps

Badger Maps is a separate field-sales mapping and routing app with CRM integrations. Public pricing checked 21 July 2026 lists Business at $58/user/month and Enterprise at $95/user/month on annual billing. Confirm the integration scope and pilot terms with the vendor.

Choose it when reps want a dedicated route-planning app and the team accepts a separate interface and CRM sync.

SPOTIO

SPOTIO is a separate platform for outside-sales and door-to-door teams. Its current pricing page asks buyers to discuss requirements, integrations, training, and implementation with sales rather than publishing a fixed seat price.

Choose it when lead management, territory work, and rep activity tracking are central to the field process.

Map My Customers

Map My Customers combines mapping, routing, and account management in a separate mobile app, with CRM integrations. Public pricing checked 21 July 2026 lists the individual plan at $55/month on annual billing and the team plan at $79/month.

Choose it when the team wants a visual field-sales workspace rather than a component inside its existing CRM.

Repsly (custom pricing)

Repsly is focused on retail execution and merchandising rather than traditional B2B field sales. It is built for CPG brands and retail service providers who need shelf audits, planogram compliance, photo capture, and in-store data collection. The mobile app includes GPS check-ins, barcode scanning, and customizable forms.

Repsly does not publish pricing. You need to contact their sales team for a quote. There is no free trial, but they offer proof-of-concept evaluations during the sales process. AI-powered image recognition for shelf audits is available as an add-on.

This is the specialist option in the list for CPG, merchandising, and retail-execution teams.

SalesRabbit

SalesRabbit focuses on door-to-door and canvassing teams. The vendor offers a free Lite version for a single user, while paid team plans and add-ons have separate commercial terms.

Choose it when the sales motion starts at the doorstep and needs territory, lead, and canvassing tools.

Tourvia (EUR 30 per licensed user per month)

Tourvia is delivered as a Salesforce managed package and works in Salesforce Mobile. Reps use their Salesforce records for accounts, contacts, opportunities, route planning, and visit logging. Route calculations process the coordinates and timing constraints described in the DPA.

Features include GPS-assisted proximity checks, nearby account discovery, multi-stop route optimization, and Salesforce Event creation. A complete 30-day trial is available on request. The subscription costs EUR 30/user/month excluding tax, billed annually.

Choose it when Salesforce is already the working interface and the team wants routes, Events, mobile visits, and reports tied to those records.

Separate app or Salesforce managed package

This choice changes the rep experience, data flow, administration, and support boundary.

Standalone apps such as Badger Maps, SPOTIO, Map My Customers, and SalesRabbit have their own interface and data model. Their CRM integrations add a separate data flow that the buyer should test and document.

CRM managed packages such as Tourvia use Salesforce records, Events, permissions, and mobile navigation. Tourvia also processes the minimum routing inputs described in its DPA. It only fits teams already running on Salesforce.

If Salesforce is the system of record and reps already use Salesforce Mobile, a managed package keeps more of the workflow in that interface. Teams on HubSpot, Zoho, or no CRM should evaluate standalone products instead.
