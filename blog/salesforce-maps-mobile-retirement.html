Salesforce will retire the standalone Salesforce Maps mobile app on August 31, 2026. Teams that use it should document the affected mobile workflow and test a replacement before that date.

What exactly is retiring?

Salesforce says the standalone Salesforce Maps mobile app will be retired on August 31, 2026. Read the Salesforce retirement notice for the current scope and migration guidance.

Why this matters for field sales teams

The impact depends on how each organisation uses the standalone app. List the affected route planning, navigation, check-in, record update, and reporting steps before selecting a replacement.

What to look for in a replacement

A good replacement should cover four things that Salesforce Maps mobile did:

  • Route optimization built from Salesforce records
  • Mobile app that works without a laptop
  • Check-in and visit reporting tied to Events or Tasks
  • Commercial terms calculated at the required licence count

Salesforce Maps alternatives that still have a mobile app

The main alternatives fall into two groups: Salesforce-native managed packages and external route planning tools.

Managed packages and separate field apps use different interfaces and data flows. Test both models with the actual Salesforce objects, mobile devices, offline conditions, and reporting requirements.

How to plan the migration before August 31

Use a controlled migration plan:

  1. Audit your current Maps usage: which users, which objects, which workflows.
  2. Identify the minimum viable route inputs: Accounts, Contacts, Leads, Opportunities, or Campaign Members.
  3. Test one alternative in a sandbox with a representative group of users.
  4. Compare the annual cost of each option against your current Salesforce Maps contract.
  5. Roll out in phases: power users first, then the rest of the team.
  6. Train reps on the new mobile app before the old one stops working.
