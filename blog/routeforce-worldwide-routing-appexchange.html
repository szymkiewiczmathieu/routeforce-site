RouteForce: Salesforce-native field execution with global routing and premium unlock

RouteForce sharpens the product around one clear promise: install the free app in Salesforce, then unlock premium capabilities for route optimization and richer field execution when your team is ready.

What RouteForce makes clearer

RouteForce is not only a mapping layer. It is a Salesforce-native field execution product built for teams that need route planning, visit execution, and business workflows inside the same environment.

The product story is easier to understand publicly: teams can install RouteForce from AppExchange, start with the free app, and unlock premium capabilities when they need more advanced execution.

Global routing remains a strong differentiator

One of RouteForce’s strongest product signals is the ability to support large-scale routing scenarios while staying native to Salesforce.

For teams planning visits across multiple territories or countries, global routing matters because field execution does not stop at a city boundary. The product story becomes stronger when route planning, visit creation, and execution all stay connected to Salesforce records.

Why the product positioning is stronger now

The clearer positioning is not only about routing. It is about the full operating model for field teams:

  • Salesforce-native workflow instead of a disconnected external tool
  • Free app entry point that reduces adoption friction
  • Premium unlock for teams that need more advanced route planning and execution
  • Field execution quality with visit reports, GPS-verified check-in, and mobile use inside Salesforce

What teams can highlight during evaluation

For a Salesforce admin or revenue operations lead, the value is straightforward: RouteForce can start as an installable native app, then expand into a broader field execution system without switching platforms.

That makes internal rollout easier to defend than products that require a separate field stack from day one.

What RouteForce supports in the field

RouteForce supports the full field workflow inside Salesforce:

  • route planning inside Salesforce
  • visit planning inside Salesforce
  • field execution inside Salesforce
  • GPS-verified check-in
  • visit reports with configurable fields
  • nearby accounts and proximity workflows
  • mobile use inside Salesforce mobile
  • configurable workflows powered by Screen Flows and Lightning Web Components

Why this matters commercially

RouteForce is easier to evaluate because the message is cleaner. Teams do not need to understand every premium feature before installing. They can start with the free app, verify the native Salesforce experience, and then unlock premium capabilities when they need route optimization and broader field execution depth.

That gives RouteForce a practical commercial bridge between first install and paid rollout.

Conclusion

RouteForce offers a clear product position: Salesforce-native route planning and field execution, global routing capacity, and a simpler adoption path from AppExchange install to premium unlock.

That makes the product easier to explain, easier to try, and easier to scale inside a Salesforce org.
