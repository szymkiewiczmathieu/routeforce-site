User Guide

A complete guide for sales reps and managers using RouteForce to plan field visits, optimize routes, and track customer interactions.

1. Getting Started

Open the App Launcher (9-dot grid icon, top-left), search for RouteForce, and click to open. The map loads with your geocoded Account markers.

Visit Alert Badge

When configured by your admin, markers display a red alert badge indicating overdue records. The badge appears when a record hasn't been visited within the configured threshold. Overdue records are also prioritized in Nearby suggestions.

Marker Tooltips

Hover over any marker to see a quick tooltip preview. Single markers show the record name. Grouped markers show the first few record names with a "+N more" count. Click the marker to open the full popup.

Marker Clustering

When many markers overlap, they cluster automatically into a circle showing the count. Zoom in to reveal individual markers.

3. Marker Popups

Click any marker to open its popup with record details and quick actions.

Popup Contents

  • Name — Clickable link to the Salesforce record
  • Address — Street, city, postal code, country
  • Owner — Clickable link to the record owner
  • Last Visit — Date of the most recent visit report
  • Phone — Clickable phone number (tap to call on mobile)
  • Custom Fields — Additional fields configured by your admin

Popup Actions

Group Popups: When multiple records share the same address, clicking the marker shows a scrollable list of all records at that location, each with its own action buttons.

4. Filtering Records

Click the filter icon on the left side of the map to open the Filter Panel. When you first open RouteForce, each object defaults to "My [Accounts/Leads/Opportunities]" — only records you own.

Account Filters

All AccountsShow all Accounts (no ownership filter)
My AccountsShow only Accounts you own
My Account TeamAccounts where you are a member of the Account Team (admin must enable)
My Role TeamAccounts owned by users in your role or subordinate roles (admin must enable)
RadiusDistance slider — only show records within X km of center

Note: Each object's ownership filter is independent. You can view "My Accounts" while simultaneously viewing "All Leads" and "My Opportunities".

After changing filters, click Apply to refresh the map.

5. Saved Filters

Save your frequently used filter combinations to reload them instantly.

  • Save: Configure your desired filters, then click Save in the Saved Filters section. Enter a name and confirm.
  • Load: Open the Saved Filters dropdown and select a saved filter — all settings are instantly applied.
  • Delete: Select the filter, click Delete, and confirm.

6. Route Planning

Click the route icon on the map toolbar to open the route planning panel.

Adding Stops

With the route panel open, click markers on the map to add them as route stops. Each step shows: step number, record name, city, record type, and clickable phone number (if contact selected).

Reordering Steps

  • Arrow buttons — use up/down arrows to move one position

7. Route Optimization

  1. Add at least 2 stops and set a starting point
  2. Click the Optimize button (blue, routing icon)
  3. Steps are reordered for the shortest total travel time

After optimization: step numbers update (green badges), arrival times appear on each step, and a route line draws on the map. The summary shows total distance and duration.

Lunch Break: The optimizer accounts for your lunch break automatically. Visits that would fall during lunch are shifted to after the break.
Step Limit: Maximum 50 stops per route. The Optimize button is disabled if you exceed this limit.

After optimization, you can still drag-and-drop steps, add/remove stops, or manually edit arrival times (click the time to override).

8. Nearby Accounts

When a route exists, the Nearby section automatically shows records within a configurable radius of your route path. Each item shows record name, type, distance from the nearest route point, and an Add button (+) to include it as a new stop.

When Visit Alert is enabled, nearby records are sorted by visit urgency — overdue records appear first.

9. Exporting & Saving Routes

  • Google Maps — opens your optimized route in Google Maps with all waypoints for turn-by-turn navigation.
  • CSV Export — downloads a spreadsheet with step, record name, city, arrival time, contact, phone, distance, and duration.
  • Create Visits — automatically creates Salesforce Events for each stop, pre-linked to the related records with optimized arrival times.
  • Save to History — saves the route for future reference. If History Naming is enabled, you can give it a custom name.

10. Events & Check-In

Loading Events

Open the Events panel (calendar icon), select a date or use Today/This Week presets, and click Load. Events appear as calendar-pin markers on the map.

Event Markers

  • Blue pulsing pin — Upcoming event (not yet checked in)
  • Gray pin with green checkmark — Checked-in event

Checking In

  1. Click Check In on an event popup
  2. Fill in the result, next action, and notes
  3. Click Confirm

This creates a Visit Report record linked to the related record and marks the event as checked in. Visit Reports are accessible from the Visit Reports tab or from the Related Lists on the Account/Lead/Opportunity record.

Previous Visit Context: The check-in modal shows the date, result, next action, and author of the last visit report for this record — so you always know what was discussed before.

11. Route History

Click the history icon (clock) to open the history panel. Past routes are listed with name, date, step count, distance, duration, and an "Optimized" badge.

  • Reload — restores all stops to the route panel, redraws the route line, and preserves optimization data.
  • Delete — click trash icon, then confirm.

12. Contact Lookup

When enabled, each route step shows a Contact dropdown. Select a contact to include them in CSV exports and calendar events — useful for knowing exactly who you'll meet at each stop.

13. Mobile Usage

RouteForce is fully responsive and works in the Salesforce Mobile app:

  • Filter panel slides out from the left (tap to open/close)
  • Route panel takes full width on small screens
  • Check-in modal adapts to the virtual keyboard
  • Map supports touch gestures (pinch to zoom, one-finger drag to pan)
  • All buttons are touch-friendly

14. Multilingual Support

RouteForce is available in English, French, and Spanish. The interface language follows your Salesforce user language setting.

To change: Settings > Language & Time Zone. After changing, do a hard refresh (Ctrl+Shift+R / Cmd+Shift+R) to reload all labels.
