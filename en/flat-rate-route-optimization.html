The per-user pricing problem

Most Salesforce route optimization tools use per-user monthly pricing — typically ranging from €50 to €150 per user per month. This pricing model creates a direct conflict between adoption and budget:

  • Growth penalty: Adding 5 new field reps means your route optimization cost jumps by €250-750/month overnight.
  • License management overhead: IT teams must track who has a license, manage onboarding/offboarding, and justify cost per seat.
  • Selective deployment: Organizations often limit route optimization to a subset of reps to control costs — reducing the overall value of the tool.
  • Budget unpredictability: As your team grows, so does your software bill — making annual budgeting unreliable.

For a team of 20 field reps at an average of €68/user/month, route optimization alone costs approximately €16,300/year. For 50 reps, that's over €40,000/year — just for route planning.

How flat-rate pricing works

A flat-rate model charges a single, fixed monthly fee regardless of how many users access the tool. RouteForce charges €249/month (billed annually at €2,988/year) for your entire Salesforce org — whether you have 5 field reps or 50.

This means:

  • Predictable budgeting: You know exactly what you'll pay this year and next year, regardless of team changes.
  • Full deployment: Every field rep gets access. No reason to limit licenses. More data, more value, better coverage.
  • No license management: No seat tracking, no user provisioning, no license reclamation when someone leaves.
  • Cost decreases per user as you grow: With 5 reps, you pay €50/user/month. With 25, it's under €10/user/month. With 50, it's under €5.

Hidden costs of per-user models

Beyond the visible per-seat fees, per-user pricing models often carry hidden costs:

  • API call limits: Some providers cap the number of route calculations per user or charge extra for premium routing. RouteForce includes unlimited calculations.
  • Third-party map fees: Solutions relying on Google Maps or similar paid services may pass through mapping API costs. RouteForce uses self-hosted open-source routing (VROOM + OpenRouteService) — no third-party fees.
  • Overage charges: Exceeding plan limits (calculations, geocoding calls) can trigger surprise invoices.
  • Annual price increases: Per-user fees tend to increase annually. A flat-rate model provides more cost stability.
  • Training per user: With per-user licensing, you may pay for training seats. RouteForce's Fully Managed plan includes team training in the setup fee.

Who benefits most from flat-rate pricing?

Growing teams

If you're planning to hire more field reps in the next 12-24 months, a flat-rate model protects you from escalating software costs.

Large field organizations

Teams with 15+ field reps see immediate savings compared to per-user pricing — often several thousand euros per year.

Multi-role access

When managers, admins and support staff also need map access, per-user pricing means paying for users who plan routes infrequently. With flat-rate, everyone has access at no extra cost.

Budget-conscious organizations

Predictable, fixed costs simplify procurement and budgeting cycles — especially valuable in organizations with strict annual budgets.
