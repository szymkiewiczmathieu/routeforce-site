What is Salesforce route planning?

Salesforce route planning is the process of organizing field visits for sales representatives directly within the Salesforce CRM platform. Instead of switching between spreadsheets, standalone mapping tools and your CRM, route planning inside Salesforce means your account data, event calendar and optimized itineraries all live in one place.

For organizations that already rely on Salesforce as their source of truth for customer relationships, native route planning eliminates data silos. Sales reps see their accounts on an interactive map, select the ones to visit, and get an optimized route — all without leaving their Salesforce environment.

A native Salesforce route planner typically uses the Account object (with Contact sub-queries) to display customer locations on a map. It calculates the most efficient visiting order considering constraints like working hours, lunch breaks and visit durations, then creates Salesforce Events automatically.

Why plan routes natively inside Salesforce?

Many field sales teams still plan their days using Google Maps, Excel spreadsheets or standalone route planning software. While these tools can calculate driving directions, they create a fundamental disconnect with your CRM data.

The problem with external route planning tools

  • Data duplication: Customer addresses must be exported from Salesforce, imported into the planning tool, then results copied back. Every step introduces errors.
  • No CRM context: External tools don't know which accounts are high-priority, when they were last visited, or who owns them in Salesforce.
  • Manual event creation: After planning a route externally, reps must manually create Salesforce events for each stop — account, contact, time, address.
  • No visibility for managers: Sales directors can't see planned routes or field coverage without asking each rep individually.

The advantages of native Salesforce route planning

  • Single source of truth: Account data, visit history, planned routes and events all live in Salesforce.
  • CRM-aware optimization: Filter accounts by last visit date, potential, territory or any custom field before planning your route.
  • One-click event creation: Optimized route → Salesforce Events created automatically with the right account, contact, time and address.
  • Permission-aware: The route planner respects Salesforce profiles, sharing rules and field-level security. Reps only see accounts they have access to.
  • Mobile-ready: Field reps access the same route planner on their phone through the Salesforce mobile app or any mobile browser.

Key capabilities of a Salesforce route planner

Not all route planning tools offer the same depth of Salesforce integration. Here are the capabilities that matter most for field sales teams:

Interactive map with account visualization

Display all Salesforce accounts on a dynamic map with smart clustering. Color-code accounts by type (prospect, client, partner) or any custom field. Click any marker to see account details without leaving the map.

Route optimization algorithm

A true route optimizer calculates the most efficient visiting sequence — not just point-to-point directions. It considers start time, end time, lunch breaks and visit durations to minimize total travel time. RouteForce uses VROOM, a proven open-source Vehicle Routing Problem (VRP) solver.

Automatic Salesforce event creation

The real productivity gain comes from creating Salesforce Events in bulk. After optimizing a route, one click creates all the calendar events with the correct account, contact, time slot and address pre-filled.

Smart filters

Filter which accounts appear on the map using any combination of Salesforce fields: territory, last visit date, account type, owner, revenue potential. Save filter combinations for daily reuse.

GPS-based nearby accounts

When a rep is in the field and has a gap in their schedule, they need to quickly find nearby accounts. GPS geolocation shows all Salesforce accounts within a configurable radius, enabling spontaneous visits.

Route history

Every planned route is saved automatically — distance, duration, stops and date. Reps can reload a previous route in one click. Managers can review field activity over time.

Export and navigation

Send the optimized route to Google Maps or Waze for turn-by-turn navigation. Export route data as CSV for reporting and analysis.
