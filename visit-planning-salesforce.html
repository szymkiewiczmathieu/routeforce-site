What is visit planning in Salesforce?

Visit planning means deciding which customers to visit, when, and in what order, then recording those visits in the Salesforce calendar. For field teams, it links commercial strategy with daily execution.

Effective visit planning in Salesforce involves three components:

  • Account selection: Identifying which accounts need a visit based on CRM data (last visit date, opportunity stage, account tier, territory)
  • Route optimization: Arranging the selected accounts in the most efficient visiting sequence to minimize travel time
  • Event creation: Recording the planned visits as Salesforce Events with the correct account, contact, time and location

When these three steps are integrated in a single tool inside Salesforce, visit planning becomes fast, data-driven and traceable.

The problem with manual visit scheduling

Without a dedicated tool, field reps often follow a tedious manual process:

  1. Review accounts in Salesforce list views or reports
  2. Identify accounts needing a visit (mental checklist or spreadsheet)
  3. Open Google Maps to estimate travel times
  4. Go back to Salesforce to create individual Events
  5. Manually enter account name, contact, time, duration and address for each event

This process takes time, creates errors, lacks geographic context, and often degrades CRM data quality.

The automated visit planning workflow

A native Salesforce visit planning tool transforms the process into a visual, streamlined workflow:

Step 1: Visualize accounts on the map

All Salesforce accounts with GPS coordinates (Latitude/Longitude) appear on an interactive map. Color-coding by account type (prospect, client, partner) or custom field values provides instant visual context. Smart clustering groups nearby accounts at higher zoom levels.

Step 2: Filter and select accounts

Use saved filter combinations to show only the accounts that matter: "Clients not visited in 30 days in my territory" or "High-potential prospects in my area." Select accounts directly on the map.

Step 3: Set visit parameters

Configure the planning parameters in the side panel:

  • Departure point (GPS position or a specific Salesforce account)
  • Date and start time
  • End of day time
  • Lunch break window and duration
  • Default visit duration per account

Step 4: Optimize the route

Click "Optimize route" and the algorithm calculates the best visiting sequence, minimizing total travel time while respecting all constraints. The optimized route appears traced on the map with numbered stops.

Step 5: Create Salesforce Events in bulk

One click creates all the Salesforce Events. Each event is pre-filled with: the correct Account, primary Contact, calculated start and end time, and the account's address. No manual data entry needed.

Bulk Salesforce Event creation

The biggest productivity gain comes from bulk event creation. Instead of creating each calendar entry individually, one click generates all planned visits as Salesforce Events.

What gets created automatically

  • Event Subject: Visit type and account name
  • Related Account: Automatically linked to the Salesforce Account record
  • Contact: Primary contact from the Account
  • Start/End Time: Calculated by the optimization algorithm based on travel time and visit duration
  • Location: Account's address

Why this matters for data quality

When creating visits becomes simple, reps do it more consistently. That means cleaner activity records, better management visibility, and more reliable follow-up data.

Smart account selection with filters

Visit planning is only as good as the accounts you choose to visit. Smart filtering ensures reps focus on the right accounts.

Common filter strategies

  • Recency-based: "Accounts not visited in the last 30/60/90 days" — ensures even coverage
  • Value-based: "High-potential accounts" or "Open opportunities > €10K" — prioritizes revenue
  • Territory-based: "Accounts in my assigned region" — keeps reps in their zone
  • Type-based: "Prospects only" or "Existing clients for renewal" — aligns with campaign goals

Saved filter combinations

Reps save their common filter combinations for instant reuse. "Monday prospecting route" or "Monthly client check-ins" become one-click presets.

Visit tracking and coverage analysis

Route history

Every planned route is automatically saved — date, distance, duration and number of stops. Reps can reload any of their last 20 routes in one click. This is useful for recurring visit patterns: reload last week's route and adjust.

Coverage visualization

The interactive map shows which areas are being covered and which are not. Managers can zoom out to see national coverage patterns or zoom in to analyze specific territories.

Salesforce reporting

Since all visits are recorded as standard Salesforce Events, your existing Salesforce reports and dashboards work out of the box. Track visit frequency per account, rep activity levels and territory coverage without any additional tooling.
